Importing from Japan to the US: Customs Duties & Tariff Guide (2026)

May 2026 · 7 min read

Japan is a popular sourcing country for luxury goods, electronics, vehicles, and collectibles. Whether you're importing a JDM car, buying vintage watches, or sourcing electronics, here's exactly what you'll pay in US customs duties in 2026.

Tariff Rates for Japanese Goods

Japan faces the same tariff structure as most non-China countries: MFN base duty + 15% Section 122 surcharge. Japan does NOT face Section 301 tariffs (those are China-only).

Popular Japan Imports: What You'll Actually Pay

🚗 JDM Car Import (25+ years old)

Vehicles 25+ years old are exempt from FMVSS compliance but still face duties.

Section 232 auto tariff: 25% on customs value (purchase price + shipping + insurance).

A $15,000 Nissan Skyline shipped for $2,000 = $17,000 customs value × 25% = $4,250 in duties.

Total landed: ~$21,250 + broker fees + EPA/DOT clearance.

⌚ Luxury Watch ($5,000 Seiko/Grand Seiko)

MFN: 6.4% + Section 122: 15% = 21.4%

$5,000 watch + $50 shipping = $5,050 × 21.4% = $1,081 in duties.

Total: ~$6,131. At this price, buying from a US authorized dealer may be comparable.

🎮 Gaming Console / Electronics ($400)

MFN: 0% + Section 122: 15% = 15%

$400 + $30 shipping = $430 × 15% = $64.50 in duties.

Total: ~$495. Many Japanese electronics have 0% MFN, so you only pay the Section 122 surcharge.

🍶 Japanese Whisky (1 bottle, $200)

Alcohol duties are complex — calculated per proof liter, not percentage. A typical 750ml bottle at 43% ABV faces approximately $2-5 in specific duty + 15% Section 122 on the total value.

$200 × 15% + ~$3 specific duty = ~$33 in duties.

Note: State alcohol taxes may also apply upon import.

The $800 De Minimis Exemption for Japan

✅ Good News

The $800 de minimis exemption is still valid for Japanese goods. If your order is under $800, it enters the US duty-free. This is different from China, where de minimis has been eliminated.

This means small purchases from Japanese retailers, Yahoo Auctions Japan, or Mercari Japan under $800 are duty-free.

Section 232 Auto Tariffs on Japanese Cars

Japanese automobiles face a 25% Section 232 tariff regardless of age. This applies to both new and used vehicles. However, Section 232 products are exempt from the 15% Section 122 surcharge — so the total rate is 25% + 2.5% MFN = 27.5%, not 42.5%.

Auto parts face a different calculation. Most auto parts are NOT covered by Section 232 and face MFN (0-4%) + Section 122 (15%) = 15-19% total. Check your specific part's HTS code.

Tips for Importing from Japan

Use a proxy buying service. For Yahoo Auctions Japan, Mercari, and Japanese-only retailers, services like Buyee, ZenMarket, or From Japan buy on your behalf and ship internationally. They handle export paperwork but NOT US import duties — you still owe those.

Check the exchange rate. The yen-to-dollar rate significantly impacts your total cost. A weak yen makes Japanese goods cheaper before tariffs.

Get proper documentation. For vehicles, you need the export certificate, bill of lading, and EPA/DOT compliance forms. Missing documents can delay customs clearance and incur storage fees.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the tariff on Japanese goods in 2026?

Most Japanese goods face MFN duty (varies by product) + 15% Section 122 surcharge. Total rates range from 15% for electronics to 47% for clothing. Automobiles face a separate 25% Section 232 tariff.

Can I import from Japan duty-free?

Yes, if your order is under $800. The de minimis exemption still applies for Japanese goods, unlike Chinese goods where it has been eliminated.

How much duty on a Japanese car import?

Japanese cars face 27.5% total (2.5% MFN + 25% Section 232). On a $15,000 vehicle plus shipping, expect $4,000-5,000+ in duties.

Is Japanese whisky taxed at import?

Yes. Alcohol faces specific duties per proof liter plus the 15% Section 122 surcharge. A typical bottle incurs $25-50 in total duties depending on value and ABV.
