How to Calculate Amazon FBA Profit Margins in 2026 (With Tariffs)

Updated April 28, 2026 · 8 min read

Most Amazon sellers calculate their profit margin wrong. They take their selling price, subtract the product cost and Amazon fees, and call it a day. But in 2026, tariffs can eat 25-60% of your product cost — and if you're not factoring that in, your "profitable" product might actually be losing money.

The Real FBA Profit Formula

Net Profit = Selling Price
- Product Cost (FOB)
- Shipping to US
- Import Duties (Tariff)
- Amazon Referral Fee (15%)
- FBA Fulfillment Fee
- PPC / Advertising Costs
- Other Costs (storage, returns, etc.)

The line most sellers miss is Import Duties. In 2026, if you source from China, your effective tariff rate ranges from 27% to 72% depending on the product category. That $5 product from Alibaba? It might cost $7-8.50 after duties — before Amazon takes their cut.

Why Tariffs Destroy FBA Margins

Let's walk through a real example. You're selling a kitchen gadget sourced from China for $29.99 on Amazon.

❌ Wrong Calculation (Ignoring Tariffs)

Selling Price: $29.99

Product Cost: $5.00 | Shipping: $1.50 | Referral (15%): $4.50 | FBA Fee: $3.50

Profit: $29.99 - $14.50 = $15.49 (51.6% margin)

✅ Correct Calculation (With 2026 Tariffs)

Selling Price: $29.99

Product Cost: $5.00 | Shipping: $1.50 | Tariff (34% on $6.50): $2.21 | Referral: $4.50 | FBA Fee: $3.50

Profit: $29.99 - $16.71 = $13.28 (44.3% margin)

That's $2.21 per unit difference — or $2,210 per 1,000 units. On higher-value products, the impact is even larger.

What Margin Should You Target?

30%+ margin: Excellent. You have room for PPC, price competition, and tariff increases. This is the target.

20-30% margin: Healthy but tight. Be careful with PPC spend and monitor tariff changes closely.

10-20% margin: Dangerous. One tariff increase or Amazon fee change could wipe out your profit. Consider raising your price or switching suppliers.

Under 10%: Not viable for most sellers. The risk-adjusted return isn't worth it.

3 Ways to Improve Your FBA Margin

1. Source from lower-tariff countries

Vietnam and India face 15-20% total tariffs vs. 40-60% for China. The product cost might be slightly higher, but the tariff savings more than compensate. Mexico under USMCA is even better — as low as 0-5% on many products.

2. Negotiate better FOB pricing

Even a $0.50 reduction in product cost saves $500 per 1,000 units. Use tools like Helium 10 to find products where the tariff math works before you commit — and reduces your tariff bill too, since duties are calculated as a percentage of product value.

3. Optimize your HTS classification

Similar products can have very different MFN rates. A product classified as a "plastic household article" at 3.4% versus a "plastic toy" at 0% makes a real difference at scale.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's a good profit margin for Amazon FBA?

Target 30% or higher after all costs including tariffs. Below 20% is risky in the current tariff environment.

Do tariffs apply to Amazon FBA products?

Yes. You pay tariffs when goods enter the US, before they reach Amazon's warehouse. The importer of record (you) is responsible.

How do I find the tariff rate for my product?

Use our FBA Profit Calculator or Tariff Calculator. For exact rates, look up your product's HTS code at hts.usitc.gov.
