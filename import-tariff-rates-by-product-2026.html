Automotive

⚠️ Section 232 Applies

Automobiles and certain auto parts face Section 232 tariffs (25%) instead of Section 122. They are exempt from the 15% Section 122 surcharge. Total auto tariff: 2.5% MFN + 25% Section 232 = 27.5%.

How to Find Your Exact Rate

The rates above are category averages. Your exact rate depends on your product's 10-digit HTS code. To find it:

Step 1: Go to hts.usitc.gov and search for your product.

Step 2: Find the most specific code that matches your product.

Step 3: Look at the "General" duty rate column — that's your MFN rate.

Step 4: Add Section 122 (15%) and Section 301 (if from China, 25%) on top.

Step 5: Or just use our tariff calculator for a quick estimate.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why do tariff rates vary so much by product?

MFN rates are set by Congress and reflect decades of trade negotiations. Products that compete with US manufacturing (clothing, footwear) have higher rates than products not made in the US (smartphones, laptops).

Are tariff rates the same for all countries?

MFN rates are the same regardless of country. But Section 301 (China only), Section 122 (exempt for USMCA), and Section 232 (specific sectors) create large differences between countries.

How do I find the exact tariff for my specific product?

Look up your HTS code at hts.usitc.gov or use our tariff calculator for a category-level estimate. For exact rates, consult a customs broker.
