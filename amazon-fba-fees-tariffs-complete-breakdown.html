Tariffs: The Cost Most Sellers Underestimate

Import tariffs are often the second-largest cost after the product itself — sometimes even larger. A $10 product from China with a 45% effective tariff rate adds $4.50 per unit in duties. At 1,000 units per month, that's $4,500 in monthly tariff costs that many sellers don't account for in their product research.

How Tariffs Are Calculated

Tariffs are assessed on the customs value (product cost + shipping + insurance), not on the Amazon selling price. Multiple tariff layers stack:

Customs Value = Product Cost + Shipping + Insurance
Total Tariff = Customs Value × (MFN Rate + Section 122 + Section 301 + Section 232)

Without tariffs, the margin would be 36%. The $2.20 per unit in duties reduces the margin by nearly 9 percentage points. At 500 units per month, that's $1,100 per month in tariff costs.

Frequently Asked Questions

What percentage does Amazon take from FBA sellers?

Amazon typically takes 30-40% of the selling price through referral fees (8-15%), FBA fulfillment fees ($3-8), and storage fees. This doesn't include tariffs or PPC costs.

Are tariffs included in Amazon's fees?

No. Tariffs are paid separately to US Customs when goods enter the country. Amazon doesn't handle import duties for FBA sellers.

How much should I budget for tariffs?

Budget 25-60% of your customs value (product cost + shipping) for tariffs on Chinese goods. For non-China goods, budget 15-33%.
