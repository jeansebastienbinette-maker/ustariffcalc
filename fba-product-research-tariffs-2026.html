FBA Product Research: Why Tariffs Must Be Your First Check in 2026

Updated April 28, 2026 · 6 min read

The #1 mistake new Amazon FBA sellers make in 2026? Finding a product with great margins on paper, ordering 1,000 units from China, and then discovering that 40% tariffs destroy their profit. Tariff analysis needs to be step one of your product research — not an afterthought.

The Old Way vs. The 2026 Way

How sellers used to do product research:

Find a product → Check demand (BSR, reviews) → Get supplier quote → Calculate margin → Order

How you should do it in 2026:

Find a product (using tools like Helium 10) → Check tariff rate by country → Get quotes from 2-3 countries → Calculate margin with tariffs → Compare landed costs → Order from lowest-cost origin

Adding the tariff check before you contact suppliers saves you from wasting time negotiating with Chinese suppliers on products where the tariff math doesn't work.

Products to Avoid in 2026

Some product categories have become nearly impossible to profit from when sourcing from China due to tariff stacking:

🚫 High-Risk Categories (China Sourcing)

Clothing & Apparel: 58%+ total tariff rate. A $5 garment costs $7.90+ after duties.

Footwear: 55%+ total tariff rate. Similar math — very thin margins.

Steel/Aluminum products: 78%+ total tariff rate. Nearly impossible.

✅ Better Options

Electronics from Vietnam: 17.5% total rate vs. 42.5% from China

Home goods from Mexico (USMCA): 4% total rate vs. 44% from China

Beauty products from India: 19% total rate vs. 44% from China

Frequently Asked Questions

Should I avoid selling products from China on Amazon?

Not necessarily. If your product has healthy margins even after 40-60% tariffs, China can still work. But always compare costs from alternative countries first.

What tariff rate makes an FBA product unprofitable?

There's no single cutoff — it depends on your selling price and product cost. Generally, if tariffs push your total COGS above 60% of your selling price, margins become dangerously thin.
