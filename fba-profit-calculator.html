Why Most FBA Profit Calculations Are Wrong in 2026

I've reviewed dozens of Amazon FBA profit calculators, and almost none of them include import tariffs. That was fine before 2018 when tariffs on most consumer goods were 0-5%. But in 2026, with effective rates of 25-60% on Chinese goods, ignoring tariffs is like ignoring your cost of goods — it makes your entire profit calculation meaningless.

Here's a real scenario I encountered: A seller found a kitchen gadget on Alibaba for $4.50, shipping $1.50, selling on Amazon for $24.99. Using a standard FBA calculator without tariffs, the margin looked like 45%. After factoring in 44% tariffs on the $6.00 customs value ($2.64 in duties), the real margin dropped to 34%. That's the difference between a product you'd aggressively invest in PPC for and one where you'd be cautious.

Understanding the FBA Cost Stack

Your actual cost per unit as an FBA seller includes layers that many beginners underestimate. The product cost (FOB price from your supplier) is just the starting point. Add shipping to US ($1-5/unit for sea freight), tariffs (25-60% of customs value from China), Amazon's referral fee (typically 15% of selling price), FBA fulfillment fee ($3-8 depending on size and weight), and PPC advertising (typically 15-25% of revenue for new products).

When you stack all of these, a product that looks profitable at the FOB price level may only yield a 10-15% net margin — and that's before returns, storage fees, and the inevitable price competition from other sellers.

The Sourcing Country Decision

One of the most impactful decisions you can make as an FBA seller in 2026 is where you source from. The tariff gap between China and alternatives like Vietnam, India, or Mexico (USMCA) can be the difference between a profitable product and a money loser.
