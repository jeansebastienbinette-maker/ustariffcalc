When Does Section 122 Expire? Exact Dates & What Comes Next

The Short Answer

Section 122 tariffs expire on July 24, 2026. That's exactly 150 days after they were imposed on February 24, 2026. The 150-day limit is written into the statute — the President cannot extend it unilaterally. Only Congress can authorize tariffs beyond this date under Section 122 authority.

February 24, 2026

July 24, 2026 — EXPIRATION

Section 122 tariffs expire automatically. Without Congressional action or new executive tariffs, the 15% surcharge disappears.

New Section 301 tariffs could take effect targeting specific countries based on the March investigations.

Can Section 122 Be Extended?

Not by the President alone. Section 122 of the Trade Act of 1974 has a hard 150-day limit. Unlike Section 301 or Section 232, which have no fixed duration, Section 122 was designed as a temporary emergency measure with built-in constraints.

Congress could extend it through legislation. Several bills have been introduced, but as of May 2026, none have advanced significantly through committee. Passing tariff legislation requires navigating complex political dynamics — some legislators support tariffs while others oppose them.

The administration could use other authorities to impose replacement tariffs. The most likely path is expanded Section 301 tariffs based on the March 2026 investigations. This would result in country-specific rates rather than the flat 15% surcharge.

What Happens the Day After Expiration?

Scenario A: No Replacement (Best Case for Importers)

If nothing replaces Section 122, the 15% surcharge simply disappears. European goods drop from ~20% to ~5%. Vietnamese goods drop from ~18% to ~3%. USMCA goods are unaffected (already exempt). Chinese goods still face 25%+ in Section 301 tariffs.

Scenario B: Section 301 Replacement (Most Likely)

New country-specific tariffs replace the flat 15%. Some countries may see rates lower than 15%, others higher. The key advantage: rates would be targeted rather than universal, creating clear sourcing winners and losers.

Scenario C: Congressional Extension (Possible)

Congress passes legislation granting broader tariff authority. The 15% could continue or be modified. This would provide the most stability but is politically uncertain.

Frequently Asked Questions

When exactly does Section 122 expire?

July 24, 2026 — exactly 150 days after the tariffs were imposed on February 24, 2026.

Can Trump extend Section 122?

No. The President cannot extend Section 122 beyond 150 days without Congressional authorization. The 150-day limit is written into the statute.

Will the 15% tariff go away completely?

It depends on what replaces it. The 15% Section 122 surcharge will expire, but new tariffs under Section 301 or Congressional legislation could maintain or increase rates for some countries.

Are USMCA goods affected by the expiration?

No. USMCA-qualifying goods from Mexico and Canada are already exempt from Section 122. The expiration doesn't affect them.

Should I import more before July 24?

Only if you believe replacement tariffs will be higher for your products. Model both scenarios before making large inventory purchases.
